William Marciel de Souza, PhD

FAPESP Postdoctoral researcher at the Virology Center Research, University of São Paulo, Brazil. He is a member of the International Committee of Taxonomy of Viruses (Peribunyaviridae and Hepeviridae Group). Dr. Souza work focuses on prospective, genomic and evolutionary studies of arboviruses and emerging zoonotic viruses.

Polianna da Silva Lemos, PhD

Graduated from the Full Degree Course in Biology (IFPA), with a master's degree in Parasitic Biology from the Amazon, from the State University of Pará (UEPA) in partnership with Instituto Evandro Chagas / SVS / MS, and a doctorate from the Postgraduate Program in Biology of Infectious and Parasitic Agents of the Institute of Biological Sciences (ICB) of the Federal University of Pará (UFPA). Currently, she is developing a post-doctoral internship at the Faculty of Public Health of the University of São Paulo (FSP-USP), under the supervision of Dr. Maria Anice Sallum and is a Technical Consultant in Vector Control of the Pan American Health Organization (PAHO). They have experience in the field of Medical Entomology, Virology, Microbiology, Genomics and Transcriptomics.

Priscilla Paschoal Barbosa, MSc

PhD student at the University of Campinas (Brazil) in the Department of Genetics, Evolution, Microbiology and Immunology. For her dissertation, Priscilla will be standardizing a low-cost molecular diagnostic method for neglected arboviruses and conducting molecular epidemiology studies on arboviruses in the tri-border area (Brazil, Paraguay and Bolivia).

Shirlene Telmos, PhD
